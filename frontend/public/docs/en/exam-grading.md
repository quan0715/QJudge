QJudge provides a powerful grading tool designed to help teachers complete a large number of evaluations in the shortest possible time.

---

## 1. Entering the Grading Console (Exam Grading)

Click the **"✓ Exam Grading"** icon in the system management sidebar to enter.

The system will load all student responses for the current contest.
- **Answer Review**: You can view the specific content of each student's response, submission time, and score overview.
- **Missing Display**: Students who have not submitted answers will be displayed as "Missing" in red, and grading cannot be performed.

---

## 2. Three-column Efficient Grading Interface

The grading interface uses a three-column design, allowing you to complete your work without frequent page switching:

| Column | By Question Mode | By Student Mode |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Left (Navigation)** | Display question list (Q1, Q2...) | Display student list (account, name) |
| **Middle (List View)** | Display responses from all students for the question | Display all question responses for the student |
| **Right (Grading Box)** | **Grading Panel**: Enter score and feedback | **Grading Panel**: Enter score and feedback |

---

## 3. Auto-grading and Manual Grading (Grading Workflow)

### Step 1: Auto-grading Objective Questions
To save time, please prioritize auto-grading:
1. Click the **"Auto-grade Objective Questions"** button in the top toolbar.
2. The system will automatically determine scores for **Single Choice, Multiple Choice, and True/False** questions based on the correct answers set in the "Question Editor".
3. Once completed, a green score label will appear in the middle column.

### Step 2: Manual Grading Subjective Questions
For Short Answer and Essay questions, manual review is required:
1. **Click Response**: Select a response marked as "Ungraded" in the middle column.
2. **Read Content**: The right column will display the student's plain text answer.
3. **Compare with Reference**: If a reference answer was set, it will be displayed below for comparison.
4. **Scoring and Feedback**: Enter the score in the score field and optional feedback in the "Comments" field (visible to students after results are published).
5. **Save and Jump**: Click **"Save"**, and the system will automatically select the next ungraded response.

---

## 4. Publishing and Withdrawing Results

By default, scores are **not visible** to students after grading. This allows teachers to verify all scores before making them public at once.

### Publishing Results
- Click **"Publish Results"** in the toolbar.
- **Student Side**: Students can see their total score, individual question scores, and teacher comments after logging in.
- **Status Display**: The label in the upper right corner will change to green **"Published"**.

### Withdrawing Results
- If you find an error and need to regrade, click **"Withdraw Results"**.
- **Student Side**: Students will no longer see their scores; it will show as "Grading in Progress".

---

## 5. FAQ

- **Q: How are Multiple Choice questions scored?**
  - A: Currently, the default is "all correct to get points". Partial credit options will be supported in the future.
- **Q: Can I modify auto-graded scores?**
  - A: Yes. Even for auto-graded questions, you can still manually enter a score in the right panel to override the system result.
- **Q: What if I want to see the specific screen of a student's answer?**
  - A: You can use the "By Student" mode, select the student, and then view their response details for each question.
